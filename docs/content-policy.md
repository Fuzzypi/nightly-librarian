# Content Policy

## Trust Contract

Source links must be preserved from input artifact to final draft output.

Every factual claim must be traceable to a source or labeled as interpretation.

## Source Fact vs. Interpretation

Use clear labels:

- Source fact: what the linked source directly says or shows.
- Interpretation: what Nightly Librarian believes it means for builders/operators.
- Action: what a builder/operator should consider doing.

Do not blur these categories.

## Labeling Rules

Rumors:

- Label as rumor or unverified.
- Do not promote as lead story unless the uncertainty is itself the story.

Benchmarks:

- Preserve benchmark source, setup, and claimed scope.
- Label vendor benchmarks, community benchmarks, and reproducible benchmarks differently.
- Do not generalize a benchmark beyond its tested conditions.

Launches:

- State what shipped, who can use it, and what is still unknown.
- Avoid vendor announcement language unless quoting and clearly marking it.

Opinions:

- Attribute opinions to the source or to Nightly Librarian interpretation.
- Do not state an opinion as settled fact.

## Editorial Shape

The public framing is:

1. what changed
2. why it matters
3. what builders/operators should do about it

## Promotion Hierarchy

- Lead story: high-confidence, high-impact, timely.
- Supporting story: useful and relevant, but not the main decision-changing item.
- Archive-only: preserved, searchable, not promoted.

## Audience Fit

Prioritize builders, operators, and small-business automation people.

Reject generic AI-news framing, empty launch coverage, and product marketing disguised as editorial judgment.

## Product Mentions

CalenCall and Veremun can appear when directly relevant, but they are downstream product funnels. They should not become the subject of every post.
