# Strategy

## Positioning

Nightly Librarian is not a generic newsletter business. It is an AI/dev intelligence and audience engine.

The content engine already exists. This repo owns distribution, publishing preparation, social generation, archive output, and operations around that digest.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- builders and operators
- technical founders
- solo developers
- small-business automation people
- people using AI tools to ship workflows, agents, and operational systems

Not the target:

- generic AI-news consumers
- VC trend watchers
- enterprise strategy readers
- people looking for entertainment-first AI coverage

## Editorial Thesis

Every public artifact should answer:

1. what changed
2. why it matters
3. what builders/operators should do about it

## Content Hierarchy

- Lead story: the one item that most changes a builder/operator decision.
- Supporting stories: useful secondary signals with clear practical impact.
- Archive-only items: preserved for completeness, search, and future reference but not promoted.

## Product-Aware Categories

- Voice AI / Realtime Agents
- AI Operations / Agent Control
- Data Infrastructure / Verification / Scraping
- Small Business Automation
- Model + API Changes
- Tools Worth Testing

## Product Relationship

CalenCall and Veremun are downstream product funnels, not the subject of every post.

They can appear when a story naturally connects to voice AI, contractor verification, small-business automation, or operational trust. They should not distort the digest into product marketing.

## Cost Discipline

Start cheap/free-first:

- static site/archive
- generated social drafts
- manual or semi-manual approval
- no paid Beehiiv or Buffer dependency until demand justifies it

Distribution automation should earn its way in through evidence, not assumption.
