Qwen 3.8 Max released as open weights (Who’s Afraid of Chinese Models?)
Alibaba released Qwen 3.8 Max as open weights, reversing its earlier decision to keep Qwen 3.7 Max closed. Simon Willison frames Ben Thompson’s argument that the US should make training-data collection explicit fair use and bar anti-distillation terms of service. Net for a builder: a new frontier open-weight model is now available to build on — verify the license terms before adopting.
Nativ: Run frontier open models locally on your Mac
Nativ is a new open-source runtime for running frontier open-weight models locally on macOS. For a solo dev already running local models via Ollama, it is worth benchmarking as a potentially simpler-UX alternative runtime. Low switching cost to try alongside an existing setup.
Kimi Work

Five US tech giants’ hidden debts soar to $1.65T on opaque AI funding
Nikkei reports the five largest US tech firms carry roughly $1.65T in off-balance-sheet or opaque debt tied to AI infrastructure buildout. A macro signal on the sustainability of AI capex, relevant to long-term platform-risk and pricing assumptions, but with no near-term action for a solo builder.
